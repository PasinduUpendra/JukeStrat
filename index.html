[jook]

(noun)

A strategic move to anticipate and neutralize an opponent’s actions or attacks. Jukes can include smart side-steps, pivots, use of the terrain, or any other action that redirects the opponent in a non-threatening direction. A successful juke often requires deep knowledge, exceptional agility, and expertise acquired through significant play.

[jookstraht]

(noun)

Employed: By forward-looking companies, universities and countries.

Christie Zwahlen
  • Bio
  • IRL

Christie is a community engagement practitioner with nearly a decade of experience developing community-based programs with institutions of higher education, nonprofit organizations, businesses and government entities. With a community engagement focus on STEAM, she developed the Bridging the Digital Divide program, which has provided over 1,500 Binghamton residents with basic computer literacy. As Statewide Director of STEAM for the Boys & Girls Clubs of Delaware, she wrote and received hundreds of thousands in grants to develop robotics, computer programming and biology programs for youth in underserved communities. She was Director of Community Engagement at Miami University from 2016-2018 where she increased the professional staff by 500% and secured over a million dollars in funding.

Christie is happiest when writing. She pursues her interests in race, gender and class through writing which blends critical race theory, gender studies, sound studies, and popular culture studies. You can read some of Christie’s writing about race and sound here.
